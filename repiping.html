Warning Signs You Need to Repipe

WHY HOMEOWNERS CHOOSE TO REPIPE

Older pipe materials degrade over time. Left unaddressed, failing pipes cause water damage, poor water quality, and skyrocketing repair costs. Know the signs.

Galvanized Steel Rusting

Galvanized pipes installed before 1980 rust from the inside out. Rust flakes restrict water flow, discolor your drinking water, and weaken pipes until they crack or burst — often inside walls where damage goes undetected for months.

Polybutylene Pipe Failures

Polybutylene (poly-b) pipes, common in homes built from 1978 to 1995, react with chlorine in municipal water and become brittle over time. They can fail without warning, leading to sudden leaks and significant interior water damage.

Low or Inconsistent Water Pressure

Mineral buildup and internal corrosion progressively narrow the interior diameter of aging pipes, reducing water pressure throughout your home. If multiple fixtures show low pressure, the issue is systemic — not a simple fixture fix.

Discolored or Foul-Smelling Water

Brown, orange, or yellow water signals heavy oxidation inside your pipes. A metallic taste or rotten-egg odor can indicate sulfur reactions or bacterial growth in deteriorated pipe walls — health concerns that repiping resolves permanently.

Know Your Options

CPVC, PEX, OR COPPER?

FlowPro uses all three pipe materials and will recommend the best fit for your home's layout, budget, and long-term goals. Every option we install is approved for South Florida's climate and water chemistry.

Material Lifespan Pros Cons Best For
CPVC 50+ years Cost-effective, easy to install, handles hot and cold water, approved for potable use More rigid than PEX; can crack if improperly supported; not ideal for freezing climates Budget-conscious homeowners needing full-home replacement quickly
PEX Recommended 50+ years Flexible routing reduces joints; highly freeze-resistant; quieter than copper; faster installation lowers labor cost Cannot be used outdoors (UV sensitive); some older well systems require flushing before use Most South Florida homes — excellent value, durability, and install speed
Copper 70–100 years Industry gold standard; naturally antimicrobial; adds home value; withstands high pressure Higher material and labor cost; susceptible to corrosion in aggressive soil conditions High-end renovations, historic homes, or buyers prioritizing maximum resale value

How We Work

OUR REPIPING PROCESS

From the moment we arrive to the final walkthrough, FlowPro follows a proven six-step process designed to minimize disruption and deliver a lasting result.

1

Water Shutoff

We isolate the main supply and confirm zero pressure throughout the system before any work begins. Safety and protection of your property come first.

2

Access Points

Technicians make precise, minimal cuts in walls, ceilings, or cabinets to reach existing pipes. We mark and protect flooring and surfaces throughout.

3

New Pipe Installation

Old pipe material is removed and replaced with your chosen material — CPVC, PEX, or copper. All connections are made to code using proper fittings and supports.

4

Pressure Test

Before restoring water service, we pressurize the entire system and hold pressure for a timed period. Any micro-leaks are found and corrected before they become problems.

5

Patch & Restore

Access openings are patched, textured, and left ready for paint. We coordinate with drywall specialists if needed to restore walls to their original condition.

6

Final Inspection

A licensed master plumber performs a complete walkthrough. We test every fixture, check all connections under pressure, and review the job with you before signing off.
