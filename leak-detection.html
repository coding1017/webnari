Our Technology

Non-Invasive Detection Methods

We locate leaks without cutting open walls or digging up your yard. Our professional-grade equipment finds the exact source so we only open what we need to fix.

Thermal Imaging Camera

Infrared thermal cameras detect temperature differences in walls, floors, and ceilings caused by moisture. Water from a hidden leak creates a distinct cold signature that shows up clearly on our high-resolution FLIR camera displays — no drilling required.

Acoustic Listening Device

Electronic ground microphones and pipe amplifiers pick up the precise sound of water escaping a pressurized line. Even a pinhole leak under a concrete slab produces a distinct acoustic signature our equipment can triangulate to within inches.

Pressure Testing

We isolate sections of your plumbing system and apply controlled air or water pressure to confirm whether a leak is present and measure its severity. Pressure decay testing is especially effective for verifying repairs after a fix is made and for pre-purchase plumbing inspections.

Video Camera Inspection

A waterproof HD camera threaded through your pipe shows the interior condition in real time. We can see cracks, corrosion, joint failures, and the exact location of a breach on a measured footage counter, so we know precisely where to dig or cut — and how deep.

What We Find

Common Leak Types We Detect

South Florida's soil conditions, aging infrastructure, and hard water create a specific set of leak patterns. Our technicians are trained to identify and locate all of them.

Slab Leaks

Water or sewer lines running beneath your concrete foundation can crack from soil shift, corrosion, or high pressure. Slab leaks are among the most destructive leak types — they erode the foundation from below and saturate your flooring before any visible signs appear. Early detection is critical.

Underground Pipe Leaks

Supply lines and sewer laterals buried in your yard are vulnerable to tree root intrusion, ground movement, and material failure over time. These leaks saturate soil, kill landscaping, and drive water bills up significantly. Our acoustic and camera equipment locates them without excavation guesswork.

Fixture and Joint Leaks

Leaks at supply valves, compression fittings, shut-off valves, and pipe unions are common in older South Florida homes. These slow drips hide inside cabinets and wall cavities, quietly soaking framing and promoting mold growth for months before the damage is discovered.

Irrigation Line Leaks

Broken sprinkler heads, cracked lateral lines, and valve box failures waste hundreds of gallons daily and spike your water bill without any visible indoor damage. We pressure-test irrigation zones and use acoustic detection to find breaks under driveways, walkways, and planted beds.

Act Now

Warning Signs You Have a Hidden Leak

Hidden leaks rarely announce themselves directly. These four warning signs indicate water is escaping somewhere in your system right now — even if you cannot see it.

Unexplained High Water Bill

A sudden spike in your water bill with no change in household usage is the most reliable indicator of a hidden leak. Even a small pinhole leak in a pressurized supply line can waste 6,000 to 10,000 gallons per month. Compare three months of bills — if the trend is upward and your habits have not changed, call us immediately.

Wet Spots on Floors or Walls

Warm or damp spots on your floor, discolored patches on drywall, or paint that is bubbling and peeling away from the surface all indicate moisture behind the finish material. In South Florida's humidity, mold can begin forming within 48 hours of a hidden water intrusion — visible wet spots mean the problem has already been building for some time.

Sound of Running Water When None Is On

If you hear hissing, trickling, or rushing water sounds when every fixture in the house is turned off, water is moving through your pipes when it should not be. Shut off all fixtures, then check your water meter — if the dial is still moving, you have an active leak that needs professional detection right away.

Low Water Pressure

A steady drop in water pressure throughout the home — not just at one fixture — often signals that water is escaping the supply system before it reaches your taps. A slab leak or underground main line break can bleed enough pressure to noticeably reduce flow at every faucet and showerhead in the house simultaneously.

Water damage is the second most common homeowners insurance claim in the United States, and the majority of those claims trace back to leaks that were present for weeks or months before they were discovered. South Florida's year-round humidity accelerates every stage of that damage — drywall absorbs moisture faster, wood framing warps more readily, and mold colonies establish in days rather than weeks.

The difference between a $400 leak detection visit and a $40,000 remediation and reconstruction project is almost always timing. When we locate a slab leak early, the repair is a targeted excavation at a single point. When the same leak runs for six months, it can undermine the foundation, saturate the subfloor, rot framing, and require a full bathroom or kitchen gut-out to correct. Call us at the first sign of a problem.
