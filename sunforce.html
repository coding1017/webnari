Will solar work during a power outage?
+

Standard grid-tied solar systems automatically shut off during outages for utility worker safety. However, systems paired with battery storage (Powerwall) can island and power your home during outages. We strongly recommend battery backup for South Florida homeowners given frequent storm-related outages.

What happens to my FPL bill after going solar?
+

You'll remain connected to the FPL grid under net metering — excess energy your system produces is sent to the grid and you receive credits. Your bill covers only the energy you consume beyond what your system produces. Most customers see bills drop to $10–$40/month (the FPL base customer charge), occasionally receiving credits.

Do I need to replace my roof before going solar?
+

If your roof has less than 10 years of useful life remaining, we recommend replacing it before solar installation (to avoid removing panels for re-roofing). We can assess your roof during the free site visit and give you an honest recommendation. We also offer coordination with roofing partners.

What is the federal solar tax credit?
+

The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) allows you to deduct 26% of your solar system cost from your federal income taxes — dollar for dollar. For a $30,000 system, that's $7,800 back at tax time. This applies to both the system and battery storage. Please consult your tax advisor for your specific situation.

Can my HOA restrict solar panels?
+

Florida's Solar Rights Law (F.S. 163.04) prohibits HOAs from banning solar panels entirely. They can impose reasonable aesthetic restrictions (like placement requirements), but cannot prevent installation outright. We handle all HOA approval processes as part of our standard service.
