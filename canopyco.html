In Miami-Dade County, permits are required for trees over a certain size (typically 4" diameter at breast height). Our arborists handle the permit application process on your behalf as part of our service — we know the local regulations inside and out and will handle all paperwork to ensure you're in compliance.
Cost varies significantly based on tree size, species, location, and access. Small trees (under 30 ft) typically range from $200–$500. Medium trees (30–60 ft) run $500–$1,200. Large trees (60+ ft) can be $1,200–$3,000+. Emergency work after storms carries a premium. We offer free on-site estimates with no obligation.
Yes — we carry $2 million in general liability insurance and full workers' compensation for all crew members. We're happy to provide certificates of insurance before any work begins. This protects both you and us, and is something you should always require from any tree company you hire.
For genuine emergencies (tree on house, blocking road, posing immediate danger), our average response time is under 2 hours, 24/7/365. During major storms like hurricanes, we pre-position crews and prioritize life-safety situations. Call (305) 555-1900 for immediate emergency dispatch.
All debris is fully removed and recycled. Wood chips go to local landscaping projects and mulch suppliers. Large logs are milled or donated to local lumber artisans. We have a zero-landfill policy. If you'd like to keep the wood chips for your garden, just let us know — no extra charge!
In South Florida, the ideal trimming window is late winter to early spring (February–April), before the hurricane season and peak growth. However, our tropical climate means trees can be safely trimmed year-round. We recommend avoiding heavy pruning during hurricane season (June–November) unless structurally necessary, as wounds heal more slowly in wet weather.
