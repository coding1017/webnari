// 04 — INSTALLATION PROTOCOL

Floor Day: Zero to Done in 8 Hours

01
// PHASE-01
Diamond Grind & Profile

Commercial diamond grinder opens the concrete profile to ICRI CSP-3. All cracks, spalls, and control joints repaired with 2-part polyurea. Floor vacuumed to 0 dust before coating.

02
// PHASE-02
Base Coat Application

Moisture-tolerant 100% solids epoxy base coat applied at 6–8 mils wet film thickness. Penetrates and bonds mechanically to the open concrete profile.

03
// PHASE-03
Broadcast & Mid Coat

Decorative vinyl flakes broadcast at full saturation into wet base coat. Mid coat squeegee-applied, locking flakes in place and filling voids for a smooth, even surface.

04
// PHASE-04
Polyurea Topcoat

Two coats of UV-stable, aliphatic polyurea applied at 3–4 mils each. Non-yellowing, abrasion-resistant, and walk-ready in 4 hours. Vehicle-ready in 24 hours.

Common Floor Coating Questions

Have a technical question our team can answer? We love talking floors.

Call a specialist
(786) 555-2050
We exclusively use diamond grinding — never acid etching, which is a cheap shortcut that produces poor adhesion. Diamond grinding opens the concrete to ICRI CSP-3 profile, which is the industry standard for coating adhesion. All cracks and damage are repaired with 2-part polyurea before any coating is applied.
Moisture is a common issue in South Florida. We use moisture-tolerant epoxy formulas that can be applied to concrete with up to 20 lbs/1000 sq ft of moisture vapor emission. In extreme cases, we apply a moisture vapor barrier primer first. We'll test your slab before starting.
Our topcoat is 100% aliphatic polyurea — UV-stable and non-yellowing. Standard epoxy topcoats will amber and fade in direct sunlight, which is why we use polyurea for every job. Your floor will maintain its color for the life of the warranty.
For our epoxy flake and metallic systems, you can walk on the floor in about 12–18 hours and drive on it after 24 hours at full cure. Our pure polyurea system is walk-ready in just 4 hours and vehicle-ready in 24. We'll give you exact times based on your system selection and ambient temperature.
A standard 2-car garage (approximately 400–500 sq ft) with our epoxy flake system typically runs $1,200–$1,800 depending on the condition of the concrete and any repairs needed. We provide free, firm quotes after an on-site measurement. No hidden fees.
Our written warranty covers delamination, peeling, and adhesion failure for 10 years from the installation date. It does not cover damage from chemical spills outside specified resistance ratings, physical damage from impacts, or normal wear on high-traffic commercial applications. We honor every claim — no questions asked.
