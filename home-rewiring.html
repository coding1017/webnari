Is Your Wiring Safe?

Does Your Home Need Rewiring?

Homes built before 1980 were wired for a fraction of today's electrical demands. Aluminum wiring was used extensively from 1965 to 1973 — it expands and contracts at a different rate than copper connectors, creating loose joints that arc and ignite. Knob-and-tube wiring has no ground wire and can't support modern loads.

If your home shows any of the signs to the right, schedule a free assessment. Most homeowners don't know the risk is there until it's too late.

Warning Indicators
⚠️

Home has aluminum wiring (common 1965–1973)

🔩

Knob-and-tube wiring visible in attic or basement

💥

Breakers trip when using multiple appliances

🔌

Only 2-prong ungrounded outlets throughout

🔥

Frequent burning smells or discolored outlets

🏗️

You're renovating or adding a major addition
