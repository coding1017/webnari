We Answer.

Standard garage door springs are rated for 10,000–15,000 cycles (one cycle = open and close). For a typical household using the door 4 times per day, that's roughly 7–10 years. High-cycle springs rated at 25,000–50,000 cycles are available and we recommend them for heavy-use doors — they last significantly longer and cost only slightly more upfront.
We strongly advise against DIY spring replacement. Torsion springs are under extreme tension — up to 300 lbs of torque — and improper handling can cause serious injury or death. This is genuinely one of the most dangerous DIY tasks for homeowners. Our techs are trained specifically for this work and carry all required safety equipment. The cost of professional repair is well worth it.
Our 21-point tune-up includes: inspection and lubrication of all moving parts (springs, rollers, hinges, cables), track alignment check, door balance test, limit switch adjustment, safety reversal test, photo eye alignment, weather seal inspection, and hardware tightening. We provide a written report of any issues found and recommendations.
Yes — we install and configure all major smart opener brands including LiftMaster myQ, Chamberlain B4505, and Genie StealthDrive Connect. These allow remote monitoring and control from your smartphone, receive alerts when the door opens or closes, and integrate with Google Home, Amazon Alexa, and Apple HomeKit. We handle the full setup and Wi-Fi configuration.

Track repair $100–$180. Full door replacement $800–$2,500+. We always provide a written flat-rate quote before any work begins — no surprises.
