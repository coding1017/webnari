These Four Situations
Are HVAC Emergencies

In South Florida, a failed AC system is more than an inconvenience — it is a health hazard. The following situations require an emergency call, not a next-day appointment.

Total AC Failure in Extreme Heat

When outdoor temperatures exceed 90 degrees — the daily norm in South Florida summers — a complete loss of cooling becomes a medical emergency within hours, especially for children, elderly occupants, and people with respiratory conditions. Do not wait for a scheduled appointment. Call now.

Water Leak or Active Flooding from Unit

A clogged condensate drain line can overflow and release gallons of water into your walls, ceilings, and flooring within hours. Left unaddressed, water intrusion causes mold growth, structural damage, and electrical hazards. If water is actively dripping from your air handler, call immediately.

Burning Smell from Air Handler or Vents

A burning or electrical odor coming from your air handler, registers, or ductwork indicates a motor overheating, a wiring fault, or a component failure. Shut off your system at the thermostat and call immediately — do not attempt to restart the unit until a technician has inspected it.

System Running Constantly with No Cooling

If your AC runs without stopping but the indoor temperature keeps rising, your system has likely lost refrigerant, has a failed compressor, or has a blockage that is causing it to work without producing results. Continued operation in this state causes further damage and escalates repair costs significantly.
