What We Do

Marine Detailing
Done Right

Every service is performed dockside or at anchor — we come to your vessel. No hauling required.

01 — Hull Services

Hull Polish & Compound

Multi-stage compound, polish, and wax process to eliminate oxidation, swirls, and chalking. Restores that factory-fresh gelcoat shine to fiberglass, aluminum, and painted hulls.

02 — Interior

Interior Restoration

Deep cleaning of cabins, helm stations, heads, and living spaces. UV protectants on all vinyl and upholstery. Odor elimination and mildew treatment included.

03 — Protection

Ceramic Coating

Professional-grade 9H nano-ceramic coating for hulls, decks, and superstructures. Hydrophobic, UV-resistant protection that lasts 3–5 years with proper maintenance.

04 — Teak

Teak Restoration

Teak scrubbing, brightening, and sealing using marine-grade products. We restore gray, weathered teak to its warm honey tone and protect it from the harsh South Florida elements.

05 — Bottom

Bottom Cleaning

Underwater hull scrubbing to remove marine growth, barnacles, and algae. Keeps your vessel performing at peak speed and efficiency. Available for trailerable and slip-kept boats.

06 — Engine Bay

Engine Bay Detail

Degreasing, cleaning, and dressing of outboard, inboard, and I/O engine compartments. Helps identify leaks and corrosion before they become major repairs.

Our Method

Four-Step Process

01

Vessel Assessment

We inspect your vessel stem-to-stern, document existing condition, and recommend a tailored service package with transparent pricing — no surprises.

How long does a full boat detail take?

It depends on the vessel size and service level. A 25-foot center console with a full exterior detail takes 4–6 hours. A 50-foot motoryacht with interior and exterior can take 10–14 hours, often split over two days. We always provide a time estimate during the assessment.

Do you service vessels while they're in the water?

Yes — most of our services are performed dockside while the vessel remains in the slip. Bottom cleaning obviously requires access to the hull below the waterline. For haul-out services, we can coordinate with local boatyards and work on your vessel in the yard.

How long does ceramic coating last on a marine vessel?

Marine ceramic coatings typically last 3–5 years depending on usage, UV exposure, and maintenance habits. We recommend an annual maintenance wash with a pH-neutral marine soap to preserve the coating's hydrophobic properties.

What marinas do you have access to?

We have working relationships with Dinner Key Marina, Bayfront Park, Haulover, Homestead Bayfront, Miami Beach Marina, Brickell Harbour, and most private yacht clubs throughout Dade and Broward counties. If we haven't been to your marina, we'll get the access we need.

Can you remove severe hull oxidation?

In most cases, yes. Heavy oxidation requires aggressive compounding followed by multiple polish stages. We'll be honest if a hull is too far gone for detailing and recommend gelcoat repair or professional spray-painting instead. We'd rather give you accurate expectations than promise results we can't deliver.
