Decision Guide

Repair or Replace?

Not every water heater problem requires a full replacement. Our technicians diagnose honestly — if a repair will extend your unit's life cost-effectively, we'll say so. Here is how we think about it.

Repair Makes Sense When...

  • Your unit is under 8 years old and otherwise functioning well
  • The issue is a failed heating element, thermostat, or anode rod
  • A faulty pilot assembly or gas valve is causing no-heat issues
  • The repair cost is less than 40% of the cost of a new unit
  • The tank shows no signs of corrosion or internal rust

Replace When...

  • Your unit is 10 or more years old — efficiency drops sharply after a decade
  • The tank is leaking from the bottom — internal corrosion is not repairable
  • Rusty or discolored hot water indicates internal tank corrosion
  • You've needed multiple repairs in the past 18 months
  • Your energy bills are climbing and you want a modern high-efficiency unit
Types We Install

Water Heater Options

We carry and install all major types of water heaters. Our technicians will help you choose the right fit for your household size, budget, and energy goals.

Traditional Tank

The most common and affordable option. Storage tank water heaters hold 30–80 gallons of preheated water, making them ideal for families with consistent hot water demand and budgets that favor lower upfront cost.

  • Lowest installation cost
  • Simple repairs, widely available parts
  • No flow rate limitations
  • Gas or electric options available

Tankless / On-Demand

Tankless units heat water only when you need it, eliminating standby energy loss. They deliver a continuous supply of hot water — no running out mid-shower — and typically last 20 or more years with proper maintenance.

  • Up to 40% more energy efficient
  • Endless hot water on demand
  • Compact wall-mounted design
  • 20+ year expected lifespan

Heat Pump Water Heater

Heat pump water heaters move heat from the surrounding air into the water rather than generating it directly — making them 3x more efficient than standard electric units. They qualify for federal energy tax credits, significantly reducing the investment payback period.

  • 300% efficiency — 3x more than electric
  • Federal tax credit eligible
  • Ideal for South Florida's warm climate
  • Annual operating savings of $300+
