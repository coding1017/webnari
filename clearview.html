Comprehensive
Inspection Services

Every inspection type follows our standardized 200+ point systematic evaluation protocol.

SEC-01
Full Home Inspection

Our flagship 200+ point evaluation covers structure, roof, electrical, HVAC, plumbing, interior, and all major systems. Delivered as a comprehensive digital report with photos, severity ratings, and contractor referrals.

SEC-02
Pre-Listing Inspection

Sell with confidence. We inspect your property before you list it, identifying issues that buyers' inspectors will flag. Gives you time to repair or disclose — and maximize your sale price.

SEC-03
New Construction Inspection

Builders make mistakes. We perform independent inspections at three phases: foundation, pre-drywall, and final walkthrough — ensuring quality before you take ownership of your new home.

SEC-04
4-Point Inspection

Insurance-required 4-point inspections covering roof, electrical, HVAC, and plumbing for older homes. Fast turnaround — typically same business day. Accepted by all major insurers in Florida.

SEC-05
Wind Mitigation Inspection

Qualify for significant insurance discounts. We document your home's wind-resistant features — roof covering, roof deck attachment, opening protection, and roof-to-wall connections — per Florida OIR requirements.

SEC-06
Re-Inspection & Follow-Up

After sellers complete requested repairs, we return to verify all items were addressed correctly. Gives you documented confirmation before closing that deficiencies have been properly remediated.

How long does a home inspection take?
+
Most single-family home inspections take 2–3.5 hours depending on size and age. A 1,500 sq ft home typically takes about 2 hours; a 4,000 sq ft older home may take 4+ hours. We never rush — we take as long as the property requires.
Should I attend the inspection?
+
Absolutely, and we strongly encourage it. Attending allows the inspector to show you findings in person and explain maintenance tips specific to your home. You'll understand the report far better — and we always take time to answer your questions during the inspection.
What if the report reveals serious problems?
+
That's exactly what the inspection is for. A clear report with significant findings gives you leverage to negotiate repairs, a price reduction, or seller credits — or to walk away if the issues are too severe. We help you understand which findings are deal-breakers and which are routine maintenance.
How soon can I book an inspection?
+
We're typically available within 24–48 hours. During peak real estate season (January–May) in South Florida, we recommend booking as soon as possible after your offer is accepted to stay within your inspection contingency window.
Do you inspect condos and townhomes?
+
Yes. We inspect all property types — single-family homes, condos, townhomes, duplexes, and small multi-family properties. For condos, we focus on the unit's interior systems, including HVAC, electrical panel, plumbing, windows, and all accessible components within the unit boundary.
