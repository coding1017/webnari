Frequently Asked

Before You Book

We believe an informed client is a comfortable client. Here are the questions we hear most often.

How much does a custom tattoo cost?
Our artists charge between $180–$220 per hour depending on specialty. Minimum session rate is $150. Small pieces (under 2 inches) are typically quoted as flat rates. Large-scale work and sleeves are priced after consultation. We never quote without seeing your vision — every piece is unique.
How far in advance should I book?
Our senior artists book 4–8 weeks in advance. Junior artists may have availability within 1–2 weeks. Walk-ins are welcome when schedules allow — call ahead to check same-day availability. For large-scale projects, we recommend booking at least 6 weeks out.
Is a consultation required before booking?
For custom pieces, yes — and the consultation is always free. For simpler designs or flash pieces, we can often proceed directly to booking. Consultations can be done in-person, over the phone, or via email with reference images. We want to make this process as easy as possible for you.
How do I prepare for my tattoo session?
Eat a full meal 2–3 hours before your appointment. Stay well hydrated. Avoid alcohol for 24 hours prior. Wear comfortable clothing that allows easy access to the tattoo area. Moisturize the area daily in the week leading up to your session. Avoid prolonged sun exposure to the skin being tattooed.
What are your sterilization and safety standards?
Iron Moth maintains the highest safety standards in the industry. All needles are single-use and disposed of in sharps containers. Work surfaces are sterile-draped for each client. Artists are licensed by the Florida Department of Health and undergo ongoing safety training. We use only reputable, skin-safe inks.
Do you do touch-ups?
Yes. All Iron Moth tattoos include one complimentary touch-up session within 6 months of the original appointment, as long as aftercare instructions were followed. Touch-ups for tattoos done elsewhere are welcome at our standard hourly rate. We stand behind every piece that leaves our studio.
