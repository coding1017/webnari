Secure Your Permits

Any plumbing, electrical, or structural work in Florida requires permits from your local building department. Unpermitted work can block home sales and void your insurance. FlowPro handles the plumbing permit on your behalf.

Finalize Your Layout and Design

Moving a toilet or drain is significantly more expensive than keeping it in place. Lock down your floor plan before any contractor begins work. Confirm rough-in dimensions for your toilet, shower pan, and vanity before purchasing any fixtures.

Select and Order Materials Early

Tile, vanities, shower systems, and specialty fixtures frequently have long lead times. Have materials on-site or confirmed in stock before scheduling your plumber and tile contractor, or delays cascade through your entire project timeline.

Protect Your Investment

WHY A LICENSED PLUMBER — NOT A HANDYMAN

Florida law requires licensed plumbers for bathroom remodel work. Beyond legal compliance, there are clear, practical reasons why hiring a licensed master plumber protects your home and your wallet.

01

Code Compliance and Permits

Licensed plumbers are the only contractors legally authorized to pull plumbing permits in Florida. Without a permit, your work will not pass inspection, cannot be financed or insured properly, and will create significant problems when you sell.

02

Liability and Insurance

If an unlicensed handyman causes a water damage event, your homeowner's insurance may deny the claim entirely. Licensed contractors carry their own general liability and workers' compensation, protecting you from out-of-pocket losses.

03

Technical Knowledge That Matters

Bathroom plumbing involves venting requirements, drain slope calculations, mixing valve pressure balancing, and water hammer prevention. These are not tasks a handyman can safely improvise — and mistakes are expensive to correct behind finished walls.

04

Warranty on Parts and Labor

FlowPro backs all installation work with a written workmanship warranty. Many fixture manufacturers also void their product warranties if the installation was not performed by a licensed plumber. We provide the documentation you need.

What Unlicensed Work Can Cost You

Homeowners frequently discover the true cost of hiring an unlicensed contractor only after the fact — when a problem surfaces, a sale falls through, or an insurance claim is denied.

  • Forced removal of finished tile and walls to access non-compliant rough-in work
  • Stop-work orders that halt your entire project mid-remodel
  • Fines levied by the county building department for unpermitted work
  • Insurance claim denials for water damage traced to unlicensed plumbing
  • Buyer requests for price reduction or repair at closing due to open permit issues
  • Personal liability for injuries to unlicensed workers on your property
