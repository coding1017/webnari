Our Philosophy

"The dojo is not a place to escape. It is a place to discover who you truly are."

At OnyxDojo, we believe martial arts transcend physical combat. Every punch, kick, and submission is a meditation on discipline, humility, and continuous improvement. Our instructors guide students through progressive mastery—not rushing strength, but building unshakeable foundation.

We honor the traditions of martial arts while embracing modern training science. Whether you're a complete beginner seeking confidence or a competitive athlete chasing excellence, you belong here. The path to mastery starts with a single step.

Frequently Asked

Questions & Answers

I've never trained martial arts before. Can I start? +

Absolutely. Every instructor here was once a beginner. We structure all fundamental classes to welcome students of any background. Start with a free trial class—no commitment.

What's the difference between classes and private training? +

Classes ($30 drop-in or $179/month unlimited) are group-based with standardized curriculum. Private sessions ($100+/hr) are one-on-one, customized to your goals—perfect for competitive preparation or accelerated learning.

Do I need to buy expensive equipment? +

Not for your first month. We provide basic gear rental. Once you're sure about training, a BJJ gi (~$80), hand wraps (~$15), and Muay Thai shorts (~$40) are minimal investments. Boxing and wrestling use basics you likely already own.

Is OnyxDojo competition-focused or casual? +

Both. We have a thriving competition team competing at local, regional, and national levels. But we equally celebrate students training purely for fitness, self-defense, or mental health. Your goals are respected.

What are class sizes typically like? +

Morning and evening peak classes run 15–25 students. Afternoon classes are smaller (8–12). We maintain student-to-instructor ratios that allow personalized correction and safety oversight.

Do you offer women-only or youth programs? +

Women make up 30% of our student body and train in all standard classes. We're planning a dedicated women's BJJ cohort launching next month. Youth (ages 6–12) have Saturday morning fundamentals. Ask about these during enrollment.
